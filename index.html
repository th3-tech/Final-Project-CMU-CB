What am I looking at?

Every protein is a chain of amino acids that folds into local shapes: α-helices (H, stabilized by local hydrogen bonds), β-strands (E, stabilized non-locally by distant partner strands), turns (T) and coil (C). Chou-Fasman reads only a short local window, so it can see what holds a helix together but is partly blind to what holds a sheet. A chameleon is a k-mer whose identical sequence appears as majority-helix in one chain and majority-strand in another — the local sequence genuinely cannot decide, so no purely local method can get both right (Kabsch & Sander, 1984).
