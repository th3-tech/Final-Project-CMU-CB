The chameleon

A protein folds into local shapes: α-helix (H, held together by local hydrogen bonds), β-strand (E, held non-locally by distant partner strands), turn (T) and coil (C).

Chou-Fasman reads only a short local window — so it can see what stabilizes a helix but is partly blind to what stabilizes a sheet.

A chameleon is a k-mer whose identical sequence appears majority-helix in one protein and majority-strand in another. The local sequence genuinely cannot decide — so no purely local method can get both right (Kabsch & Sander, 1984).
